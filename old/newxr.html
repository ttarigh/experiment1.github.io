I spearheaded the development of Aesthetic Computer's innovative Happy Hands project, which involved creating a hands-free controller built upon Google's powerful MediaPipe platform, all implemented in JavaScript. My dedication to this endeavor led to the contribution of thousands of lines of code, driving the project's success. At present, we're in the process of crafting and blueprinting interactive augmented reality games that rely on hand gestures as the primary input method, opening up new realms of immersive gameplay. If you're eager to explore the capabilities of hand gesture mapping, check out our offerings at these links: for mapping hand gestures, visit https://aesthetic.computer/wave. For the ability to communicate through hand gestures and camera input, head over to https://aesthetic.computer/handtime. And if you're up for the challenge of a pinball game controlled solely by hand gestures, dive into the action at https://aesthetic.computer/staka. Join us in this exciting journey into the future of interactive technology!
