"Ready Companion" - Augmented Reality Campus Tour Guide App

As the lead software engineer behind "Ready Companion," I embarked on a journey to create an innovative augmented reality (AR) mobile application tailored for iPhone users. Tasked with translating a visionary concept into a functional reality, I closely collaborated with the startup's founder to bring this cutting-edge project to life.

"Ready Companion" is a pioneering campus tour guide application specifically designed to cater to the needs of college students. It harnesses the capabilities of Swift, RealityKit, and ARKit technologies to provide an immersive and interactive experience. By blending augmented reality with cutting-edge mobile technology, this app redefines the way students explore and interact with their campus surroundings.

This version of "Ready Companion" represents our initial iteration, but we are already working on the next evolution. Our team is currently exploring the incorporation of 3D assets into geolocked spaces, pushing the boundaries of AR experiences. We're delving into the application of advanced tools, including shader languages, to further enhance the app's visual and interactive elements. This next phase aims to provide users with an even more immersive and dynamic campus exploration experience, marking an exciting evolution in the project's ongoing development.

Spoons: VR Lupus Simulator

I collaborated with a colleague to develop an immersive software simulator based on Miserandino‚Äôs Spoon Theory to raise awareness for the unseen symptoms of chronic illness using Unity Engine, C# scripting, and Blender. The five-minute experience simulates the difficulty of doing the dishes, cleaning a room, and going to classes equipped with the lull of never-ending doctor‚Äôs voicemails in the background. It begins with a title sequence explaining SPOONs theory. We wrote C# scripts that automatically move the user from title sequence to the scene. JOBMII

JOBMII is a personal project and augmented reality performance art piece that I have planned for 2023. Using QR-codes, JOBMII will be a small 3D figure of oneself that can be placed on resumes. The job market was especially difficult in the last year, and many of my peers felt extreme desperation to lock some kind of job down. Especially for my peers who relied on obtaining a job to renew their immigration status, the relentless applying was dire. At first, JOBMII was meant to have practical applications. The small QR code could launch a render that would be animated and could speak about one‚Äôs job experiences. There would be a link that could take an employer to a calendar to set up a meeting. I believe for my own personal use, I will still implement it this way. But, as I thought about it more and more, I decided to turn this once helpful idea into something drastic: by making the 3D avatar respond negatively to a lack of attention. The avatar, after politely pitching itself, would start to get louder, more obnoxious, and cry/roll around around the resume, pleading and begging for a job. The heart of this project is to force the employer to literally turn off their phone/exit the device to ignore the palpable desperation in people‚Äôs voices to be employed. I am excited to implement this piece in the near future. .
